## 1) Problem (Reality We Are Solving)

### Student side

- Students often optimize for grades instead of real learning.
    
- Progress is not visible, so motivation drops.
    
- Many engage only with subjects they already like or understand.
    
- AI/LLM usage makes outcome-based assessment less meaningful.
    

### Teacher side

- Engagement is hard to maintain consistently.
    
- Extra workflow is a dealbreaker.
    
- Existing systems focus on grades, not learning behavior.
    

### School / University side (Buyer)

- They have data but lack clarity.
    
- Hard to detect disengagement early.
    
- KPI reporting exists, but insight into student momentum is weak.
    

---

## 2) Core Insight

Grades show results, but they hide progress.

There is a gap between:

- how students experience learning (emotional, motivational)
    
- how institutions measure learning (administrative, numeric)
    

The product bridges this gap by making learning effort and growth visible.

---

## 3) Product Positioning

**Student Progress Operating Layer** on top of existing systems (e.g., Kundelik).

Not a replacement for LMS or digital diaries.

Primary value:

- Turn existing academic data into visible growth and engagement signals.
    

---

## 4) Core Metrics (Concept Level)

### Engagement Index (behavior signal)

Measured from observable behavior:

- Attendance consistency
    
- Assignment completion rate
    
- Submission timing (on-time vs late)
    
- Participation signals (when available)
    
- Improvement trend
    

Purpose:

- Show whether students are actively participating in learning.
    

### Growth Score (progress signal)

Measures change over time, not absolute grades:

- Improvement vs personal baseline
    
- Consistency of improvement
    
- Progress trajectory
    

Purpose:

- Show whether effort is translating into progress.
    

### Relationship

Engagement drives growth.  
Together they form a clear picture of learning momentum.

---

## 5) Key Product Principles

1. Zero extra work for teachers.
    
    - Teachers continue using current workflows.
        
    - Platform acts as a layer.
        
2. Behavior visibility over gamification gimmicks.
    
    - Gamification is UX, not the product.
        
3. Effort should be visible.
    
    - Students see progress, not just grades.
        
4. Actionable insights for institutions.
    
    - Metrics must support decisions, not just dashboards.
        

---

## 6) Integration Strategy (Kundelik Context)

Long-term: integrate with Kundelik as a layer.

But NOT required for MVP.

Phases:

1. MVP: manual or lightweight data input (CSV/import/simple panel).
    
2. Pilot: semi-automated sync.
    
3. Scale: full integration.
    

Reason:

- Avoid dependency on external bureaucracy early.
    

---

## 7) What Changes After Adoption (Core Value)

Before:

- Schools rely on grades + intuition.
    
- Student motivation is hard to measure.
    

After:

- Schools track engagement and growth in real time.
    
- Early warning signals for disengagement.
    
- Students see visible progress.
    

---

## 8) Positioning Statements (Working Versions)

Institution-facing:

- "Grades show results, but they hide progress. We help schools visualize student growth and engagement in real time."
    
- "Track student engagement and growth in real time — instead of relying only on grades and intuition."
    

Student-facing:

- "Your effort should feel like progress, not just numbers in a gradebook."
    

AI-era framing:

- "In the AI era, outcomes are easy to generate — real progress is harder to see. We make learning growth visible again."

# Landing Page Plan (Execution Blueprint)

## Goal of Landing Page

Validate demand and collect high-quality signals from two audiences:

- Institutions (schools / universities)
    
- Students
    

Primary success signals:

- Institutions request pilot conversations.
    
- Students join waitlist voluntarily.
    

---

## 1) Entry Split (First Screen)

Question:  
"Who are you?"

Two clear paths:

- For Schools & Universities
    
- For Students
    

Do not mix messaging after the split.

---

### Hero

Headline:

- "Make student growth visible."
    

Subheadline:

- Track engagement and learning momentum without changing teacher workflows.
    

CTA:

- Join pilot / Request early access

- Works alongside existing systems like Kundelik.
    

---

### Problem Section

- Grades show outcomes, not engagement.
    
- Disengagement is hard to detect early.
    
- Reporting exists, but insights are limited.
    

---

- Engagement index dashboard

- Early warning indicators
    

---

For Administrators:

- Clear engagement analytics
    
- Early intervention signals
    

For Teachers:

- No additional workload

- Better decisions with real-time insight

Strong signals:

- Institution asks for meeting or pilot.
    
- Students join without incentives.
    
- People describe real pain in detail.
    

Weak signals:

- Likes or generic praise.

- Show engagement index + growth score.
    
- Simple student progress view.

- Validate whether metrics feel accurate and useful.
